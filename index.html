IGS 2016 also serves as the 4th GOCI PI Workshop and the 2nd GOCI-II Workshop, Since the successful launch of COMS/GOCI, the first-ever ocean color satellite on the geostationary orbit, Korea Ocean Satellite Center (KOSC) in KIOST has hosted several international conferences to foster the application of geostationary ocean color data to various scientific fields. By combining GOCI PI Workshop and GOCI-II Workshop with IGS 2016, we expect that IGS 2016 encompass broader topics that are related to GOCI/GOCI-II and other ocean color sensors, such as Cal/Val activities, ocean optics, new satellite missions, algorithm development, and applications to ocean · atmosphere · terrestrial sciences. About IGS 2016

27-30 Sept. 2016 Jeju

 The International Geostationary Ocean Color Imager (GOCI) Symposium 2016 (IGS 2016) will be held from 27 to 30 September 2016 at Phoenix Island Resort, Jeju Island, Republic of Korea. The IGS 2016 meeting is organized by Korea Ocean Satellite Center (KOSC) of Korea Institute of Ocean Science and Technology with support from Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries.

 Following the previous GOCI PI and GOCI-II workshops, the IGS 2016 meeting aims to exchange recent research results on geostationary ocean color observations and to prepare for future geostationary missions such as GOCI-II. GOCI is currently the only ocean color sensor operating on a geostationary orbit, providing eight images a day of the north-east Asian seas around Korea.

 The data quality and data service have been continuously improved since the first operational service in April, 2011. GOCI data have been used for applications in diverse fields in ocean, atmosphere and land, and have been explored by many researchers from countries in the region but also from America and Europe. Recently, the KORUS-OC campaign took place in May-June 2016 to evaluate the strengths and limitations of the GOCI sensor.

 Development of the GOCI-II sensor and the ground data processing system is well underway for launch in early 2019.
